== Operator Precedence

This appendix provides a quick-reference for all Tiri operators, ordered from highest to lowest precedence.  Operators on the same row share the same precedence level.  See <<ch-operators>> for full semantics.

=== Precedence Table

[cols="1,4,2"]
|===
|Level |Operators |Associativity
|10|`pass:[**]`|Right
|9|`-` (unary), `not`, `#`, `~` (unary)|Unary (right)
|8|`*`, `/`, `%`|Left
|7|`+`, `-`|Left
|6|`..`, `<<`, `>>`|`..` right; shifts left
|5|`&`|Left
|4|`^`, `is`, `!=`, `<`, `>`, `\<=`, `>=`, `in` |Left
|3|`\|`, `and`, `\|>`|Left
|2|`or`, `??`, `? :>`|Left (`? :>` right)
|===

=== Postfix and Suffix Operators

Postfix operators (`++`, `??` postfix) and suffix chains (`.field`, `[index]`, `()`, `?.`, `?:`, `?[]`) bind tighter than all binary operators.  They evaluate immediately after the primary expression, left to right.

=== Compound Assignment Operators

Compound assignment operators (`+=`, `-=`, `*=`, `/=`, `%=`, `..=`, `?=`, `??=`) are statements, not expressions.  They do not participate in precedence evaluation.

=== Parentheses

Parentheses override precedence.  `(a + b) * c` forces addition before multiplication.

=== Examples

[source,tiri]
----
2 + 3 * 4           -- 14 (level 8 before level 7)
2 ** 3 ** 2         -- 512 (right-associative: 2 ** (3 ** 2))
'a' .. 'b' .. 'c'   -- 'abc' (right-associative)
x > 0 and x < 10    -- comparison (level 4) before logical (level 3)
a & 0xFF | 0x100    -- AND (level 5) before OR (level 3)
a ?? b or c         -- ?? and or share level 2; evaluates left to right
x ? a :> y ? b :> c -- right-associative: x ? a :> (y ? b :> c)
----
